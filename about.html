About the Sounds and the City project


The project is about noise pollution and its direct relation to elevated heart rates. The science has been done and this project will further visualize the hypothesis to viewers of the sounds-and-the-city web page. Mark Carles has a resting heart rate of about 70 bpm and normal stress levels. The project will be showing certain sections of Manhattan at certain times of day--measuring the decibal levels of the area, with video of what I am seeing and hearing, and finally, the heart rate waves during the duration of the instance. All to be done in one day during a tour of certain Manhattan neighborhoods that I normally traverse. Sound scapes that are normally witnessed by native New Yorkers everyday and the project will show the potential hazards of noise pollution on the noises.
